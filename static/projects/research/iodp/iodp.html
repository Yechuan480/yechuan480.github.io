Sample and Geological Background

The International Ocean Discovery Program (IODP) retrieves core samples from the ocean floor, offering insights into Earth's tectonic and climatic history. My research utilizes samples from various drilling sites to study mantle-derived minerals.

These samples, often basaltic or ultramafic in composition, provide a window into the geological processes of ocean crust formation and mantle dynamics.

Cross-section of an IODP core showing mineral layering

Nominally Anhydrous Minerals

Nominally Anhydrous Minerals (NAMs) such as olivine and pyroxene typically contain trace amounts of water as hydroxyl groups. My work focuses on quantifying this water content to understand its role in mantle processes.

Water in NAMs influences mantle viscosity and melting behavior, impacting the global water cycle and volcanic activity.

Current Work

I am using Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR) to measure water content in NAMs from IODP samples. Additionally, Electron Probe Microanalysis (EPMA) is applied to determine trace element compositions.

Preliminary data suggest variations in water content linked to depth and tectonic setting, with ongoing analysis to refine these observations.
